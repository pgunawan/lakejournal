Lake Issue #8 was our last printed volume. However, we are currently planning to relaunch Lake as an online journal, likely in early 2014. We found that subscriptions and advertising could not support a small magazine like Lake, which was devoted to high quality work. Printing costs alone run into the thousands of dollars for even small print runs of 300 – 400 copies. Like most cultural journals, Lake relied on grants and donations to make up the shortfall between revenues and expenses.

We would welcome interested people to join Lake Publishing Society as a member to help us plan for the future:

Don't Say It

You admire the wild grasses
for their reticence.
When you cut across the dusk for home,
the meadow is more beautiful
for all it keeps inside it.
Syllables of seeds catch in your socks
but they don’t need to say
Thank you, friend,
even if you’ve carried them
for miles.

                        by Lorna Crozier
